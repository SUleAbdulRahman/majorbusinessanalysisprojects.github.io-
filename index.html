UNILEVER LONDON CANTEEN ORDERING SYSTEM

Transforming Lunch Breaks: The Unilever Canteen Innovation Story At Unilever’s bustling UK offices, lunch hour had become a daily bottleneck. With over 1,500 employees navigating just two canteens, long queues, limited food choices, and wasted employee time were the norm. Meals were often out of stock, while others were discarded uneaten. Employees were frustrated. Productivity was taking a hit. The vision: a smarter, faster, AI-powered Canteen Ordering System. By allowing employees to pre-order meals, access an interactive daily menu, and have food delivered directly to their desks, the new system tackled the root causes of canteen chaos. AI-driven Smart Inventory Management ensured just the right amount of food was prepared—nothing more, nothing less. And with seamless payroll integration, no payments or transactions slowed down the process. The result? A projected $375,000 annual savings, a 70% drop in wait times, and a dramatic boost in employee satisfaction. This case study isn’t just about solving a problem—it’s about redefining operational efficiency and employee experience through innovation. Turning the Wheels of Quality at AutoMotion Manufacturing Company

At AutoMotion Manufacturing, a once-reliable brand in the automotive market, a troubling pattern had emerged—vehicles were rolling off the production line riddled with defects. From misaligned panels to malfunctioning electronics, the issues were not only hurting the bottom line but threatening the company’s reputation for quality and safety. Concerned by a steep decline in sales and a surge in customer complaints, leadership launched a deep-dive investigation using the Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram. What they uncovered was eye-opening: a tangled web of root causes across six key areas—People, Methods, Machines, Materials, Measurement, and Environment. Lack of training and motivation among staff, outdated machinery, inconsistent supplier materials, infrequent quality checks, and unstable factory conditions were just the tip of the iceberg. But the turning point came when cross-functional teams took targeted actions based on these insights—revamping quality inspection routines, investing in workforce development, upgrading equipment, and reinforcing a culture of excellence. The result? A dramatic reduction in defect rates, revitalized employee morale, and a 30% rebound in customer satisfaction within months. AutoMotion didn’t just fix a quality issue—they reignited a commitment to craftsmanship that now drives them forward. This case study isn’t just about fixing flaws—it’s about transforming a business through data, insight, and decisive leadership.Tools and Techniques:Gap Analysis/Fishbone Diagram, Requirment Gathering and Elicitation,Stakeholder Engagement,Document Analysis,Data Analysis,MS Excel,Critical thinking
